## ğŸ“ How to Use

### For Self-Study

1. **Week 1-2:** Complete Breakthrough Workout + Model Problems
2. **Week 3-4:** Try problems from `by-difficulty/foundation/`
3. **Week 5-8:** Progress through intermediate and advanced
4. **Week 9+:** Challenge yourself with Round 2 problems

### For Teaching

1. Use **seasonal-puzzles/** to introduce linguistic thinking
2. Assign **model-problems/** with guided instruction
3. Create custom problem sets using the **database** spreadsheet
4. Mix difficulty levels for differentiation

### For Competition Prep

1. Complete all problems at your target level
2. Time yourself (Round 1: 3 hours, Round 2: 4 hours)
3. Focus on weak areas identified in the database
4. Work through recent years (2020-2022) for modern format

## ğŸ“ˆ Typical Problem Structure

1. **Introduction** - Brief background about the language
2. **Data** - Words, sentences, translations, or patterns
3. **Questions** (3-5) - What you need to figure out
4. **Solutions** - Detailed explanations (separate or combined)

**Common Question Types:**
- Translate new sentences
- Find the pattern/rule
- Fill in missing forms
- Explain why something works this way

## ğŸ† Competition Format

### Round 1 (Open Round)
- **Time:** 3 hours
- **Problems:** 10 problems
- **Levels:** Mix of Breakthrough, Foundation, Intermediate, Advanced
- **Format:** Multiple choice and short answer
- **Eligibility:** Open to all students

### Round 2 (Invitational)
- **Time:** 4 hours
- **Problems:** 5 very challenging problems
- **Difficulty:** All problems are Advanced/R2 level
- **Format:** Extended response with full explanations
- **Eligibility:** Top ~10% from Round 1

- `-questions` = Problem only
