## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Possessive Marking**: Understanding different possessive suffixes
2. **Alienable vs. Inalienable Possession**: Distinguishing inherent vs. acquired possession
3. **Case System**: Recognizing genitive and other case markers
4. **Morphological Complexity**: Analyzing multi-layered possessive constructions

## Key Patterns

### Possessive Suffixes:
- **-ni**: 3rd person singular possessive (alienable - "his/her X")
- **-i**: 1st person singular possessive (alienable - "my X")
- **-u**: 2nd person possessive (alienable - "your X")
- **-ŋi:**: Inalienable possession marker (body parts, inherent relations)

### Alienable vs. Inalienable:
- **Alienable**: things that can be owned/acquired (money, trees, animals)
  - Use: -ni, -i, -u
- **Inalienable**: body parts, inherent attributes (head, shoulder, tail)
  - Use: -ŋi:

### Personal Pronouns:
- **bi**: I, my
- **si**: you (singular), your
- **su**: you (plural), your

### Genitive Construction:
For complex possession ("X's Y's Z"):
- Possessor takes genitive marker **-ŋi-** before next possessive
- Example: "my son's shoulder" = bi b'ataŋi: bogdoloni
