## Problem Description

The plural of Catalan nouns is usually formed by adding the ending -s. But if the noun ends in one of the letters s, x or ç, more complex rules apply.

Here are the singular and the plural forms of some Catalan nouns (in simplified spelling) and their English translations. Some forms are missing.

### Tasks:
- **Assignment 1**: Fill the gaps. Explain your solution.
- **Assignment 2**: Find out when stress marks (` and ´) are used.
- **Assignment 3**: Which syllable is stressed in words where there is no ` or ´ mark?

## Key Patterns

### Plural Formation Rules:
For nouns ending in **s, x, ç**:
1. If stressed on final syllable: add **-os**
   - Example: permís → permisos (permission)
2. If not stressed on final syllable: no change
   - Example: càries → càries (caries)
3. Special patterns for different consonant endings

### Stress Marking:
- **Grave accent (`)**:  indicates stress on non-final syllable with open vowel
- **Acute accent (´)**: indicates stress on non-final syllable with close vowel
- **Unmarked**: stress follows default patterns

### Default Stress (unmarked words):
- Likely penultimate (second-to-last) syllable for most words
- Or final syllable in certain conditions
