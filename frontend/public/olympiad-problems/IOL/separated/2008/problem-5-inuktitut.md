b) Translate 5 English sentences to Inuktitut

## Key Linguistic Features

### Ergativity
- **Intransitive subjects**: Absolutive case (*-Ø*)
- **Transitive subjects**: Ergative case (*-up*)
- **Objects**: Absolutive case

### Person Marking on Verbs
- Subject agreement markers
- Object agreement markers (for transitives)
- Different patterns for intransitive vs transitive

### Case System
- Absolutive: *-Ø* (unmarked)
- Ergative: *-up*/*-uup*
- Modalis: *-mik* (indefinite object, "a/some")
- Possessive: *-vit* (your), *-it* (your, plural possessor)

## Morpheme Breakdown Examples

- *takujaatit* = taku-jaa-tit (see-PAST-3sg→2sg) "saw you"
- *qukiqtanga* = quki-q-tanga (shoot-PAST-3sg→3sg) "shot him"
- *aarqisijuq* = aarqi-si-juq (cure-PAST-3sg.INTR) "cured"

## Solution Strategy

1. **Identify case markers** on nouns
   - Ergative *-up*: marks transitive subjects
   - Absolutive *-Ø*: marks objects and intransitive subjects
   - Modalis *-mik*: marks indefinite objects

2. **Analyze verb agreement**
   - Transitive: subject + object marking
   - Intransitive: subject marking only
   - Different suffixes for 1sg, 2sg, 3sg, etc.

3. **Build paradigm tables**
   - Transitive: 3→3, 3→2, 3→1, 2→3, 1→3, etc.
   - Intransitive: 1sg, 2sg, 3sg

4. **Note possessive marking**
   - *-vit* = your (sg)
   - *-it* = your (with plural possessor)

## Why Difficult? (4/5 stars)

- **Ergative-absolutive alignment** unfamiliar to English speakers
- **Complex verb morphology** with portmanteau morphemes
- **Multiple interacting systems** (case, agreement, possession)
- **Subtle distinctions**: definite vs indefinite objects
- **Inverse marking**: Some verbs show "inverse" patterns

## Key Discoveries Needed

1. Ergative case marking pattern
2. Verb agreement paradigms
3. Modalis case for indefinite objects
4. Possessive suffixes on nouns
5. Intransitive vs transitive verb forms
