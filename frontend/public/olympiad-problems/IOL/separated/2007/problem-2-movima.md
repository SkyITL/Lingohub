### Tasks

**§1.** Instead of standing for a sequence of two sounds, a certain combination of two letters always stands for a single specific consonant in Movima. Which combination is this?

**§2.** Fill in the gaps.

## Notes

- y reads as y in *yahoo*
- č as ch in *church*
- ɬ, ŋ, ' are specific consonants of Movima
- A colon after a vowel indicates length
- A tipoy is a long sleeveless chemise worn by Indian women

## Linguistic Insights

### Key Patterns

1. **Negation Formation**:
   - Prefix "kas" added
   - Infix "a'" inserted after stressed syllable
   - Stress pattern changes systematically

2. **Consonant Clusters**:
   - Certain letter combinations represent single sounds
   - Need to identify digraphs vs. clusters

3. **Vowel Length**:
   - Length marked with colon
   - May interact with stress and negation

4. **Stress Assignment**:
   - Appears to be predictable
   - Interacts with negation morphology

### Difficulty Analysis

**Difficulty Level**: 3/5 (Moderate-Challenging)

**Challenge Areas**:
- Identifying the syllable where negation infix is inserted
- Understanding stress patterns
- Distinguishing digraphs from consonant sequences
- Working backwards from negative to base forms

**Solution Approach**:
1. Analyze where "a'" is inserted in each word
2. Look for phonological patterns (syllable structure, stress)
3. Identify the digraph that represents a single consonant
4. Apply discovered rules to fill gaps
5. Verify consistency across all examples
