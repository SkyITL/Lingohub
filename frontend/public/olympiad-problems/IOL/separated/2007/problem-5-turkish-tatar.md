## Notes

- The letters ä, ı, ö, ü stand for specific vowels (the first two are not unlike the ones in *cat* and *bird*, respectively)
- ğ is a specific Turkish consonant
- c, ç, ş, y are pronounced as the initial consonants in *jet*, *chip*, *ship*, *yet*
- Boza is a weakly alcoholic drink made from millet

## Linguistic Insights

### Key Patterns

1. **Initial Consonant Correspondences**:
   - Turkish b- ↔ Tatar m- (in some environments)
   - Turkish y- ↔ Tatar varying patterns

2. **Vowel Correspondences**:
   - Systematic vowel shifts between languages
   - Vowel harmony patterns may differ

3. **Medial Consonant Changes**:
   - Different reflexes of historical consonants
   - Consonant weakening or strengthening

4. **Word-Final Patterns**:
   - Different morphological endings
   - Systematic sound changes in final position

### Difficulty Analysis

**Difficulty Level**: 3/5 (Moderate-Challenging)

**Challenge Areas**:
- Identifying systematic vs. sporadic changes
- Handling multiple possible correspondences
- Dealing with borrowed vs. inherited words
- Predicting changes in unseen words

**Solution Approach**:
1. Identify regular sound correspondences
2. Look for patterns by position (initial, medial, final)
3. Consider vowel and consonant changes separately
4. Apply discovered rules to fill gaps
5. Verify consistency across all pairs
