## Problem Description

Several Lango words and phrases are given with their unordered translations.

### Given Data:
- **dyè òt**, **dyè tyèn**, **gìn**, **gìn wìc**, **nìg**, **nìg wàn**, **òt cèm**, **wìc òt**
- Translations: eyeball, grain, roof, garment, floor, restaurant, sole of foot, hat

### Tasks:
- **Assignment 1**: Pair up the words with their correct translations.
- **Assignment 2**: Translate into English: **cèm, dyè**.
- **Assignment 3**: Translate into Lango: **window**.

## Notes

- **ŋ** and **n** are specific consonants of the Lango language
- **t** and **ɔ** are specific vowels of the Lango language
- The marks **«`»** and **«´»** indicate tones (a higher or lower level of the voice during pronunciation of the syllable)
- **«`»** = low tone (falling)
- **«´»** = high tone (rising)

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Tone Recognition**: Understanding how tone changes word meaning
2. **Compound Analysis**: Breaking down compound words into components
3. **Semantic Relationships**: Understanding how body parts and objects combine
4. **Pattern Matching**: Using logical deduction to match words with translations

## Key Patterns

### Word Components:
Based on the translations and compound structure:
- **dyè** = grain (appears in "dyè òt", "dyè tyèn")
- **òt** = roof/top (appears in "dyè òt", "wìc òt", "òt cèm")
- **tyèn** = related to "grain"
- **gìn** = thing/object (appears in "gìn", "gìn wìc")
- **wìc** = head/top part (appears in "gìn wìc", "wìc òt")
- **nìg** = foot/bottom (appears in "nìg", "nìg wàn")
- **wàn** = sole/bottom
- **cèm** = house/building (appears in "òt cèm")

### Compound Structure:
Lango uses compound words where:
- X + Y often means "Y of X" or "X's Y"
- Body part terms combine with object terms
- **dyè òt** = grain + roof = eyeball (round like grain, on top like roof)
- **wìc òt** = head + roof = hat (covers head like roof)
- **gìn wìc** = thing + head = hat (thing for head)
- **nìg wàn** = foot + sole = sole of foot
- **òt cèm** = roof + house = roof

### Tone System:
- Low tone (grave accent `): indicates falling pitch
- High tone (acute accent ´): indicates rising pitch
- Tone is lexically contrastive (changes word meaning)
