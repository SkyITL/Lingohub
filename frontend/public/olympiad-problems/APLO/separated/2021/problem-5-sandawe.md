## Notes

- **x**, **th**, **tsh**, **kh**, **k'**, **ŋ**, **ŋ!**, **ʔ**, **!'** and **ǁ'** are consonants
- Tone marks: **̀** (low), **́** (high), **ˇ** (rising: low ↗ high)
- Circle under vowel (ḁ) = devoiced vowel
- **M** = masculine; **F** = feminine
- Click consonants present

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Gender System**: Masculine/feminine agreement on nouns and verbs
2. **Evidential Particles**: Marking of information source ("apparently")
3. **Three-Tone System**: Low, high, and rising tones
4. **Click Consonants**: Rare click sounds (!', ǁ', ŋ!)
5. **Complex Morphology**: Multiple interacting grammatical categories

## Key Patterns

### Gender Marking:
- Masculine: -sò, -ỳ endings
- Feminine: -sù, -è endings
- Agreement on subject, object, and verbs

### Evidentiality:
- **-gé** suffix: "apparently" (reportative/inferential)
- Marks indirect evidence

### Negation:
- **-tshó**, **-tshú** suffixes

### Tone Patterns:
- Systematic tone changes based on grammatical function
- Tone interacts with gender and evidentiality

## Difficulty Analysis

**Level**: Intermediate-Advanced

**Reasoning**:
- Multiple interacting systems (gender + evidentiality + tone)
- Click consonants are unfamiliar
- Three-tone system requires careful attention
- Gender agreement across multiple elements
- Evidential marking adds semantic complexity
- One of the most phonologically complex APLO problems
