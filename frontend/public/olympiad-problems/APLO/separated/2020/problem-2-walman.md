## Problem Description

Sentences showing complex coordination patterns with verb agreement for gender and number.

### Tasks:
- (a) Translate into English in all possible ways
- (b) Translate into Walman

## Notes

- **w**, **y** and **ch** are consonants
- A **cassowary** is a flightless bird native to New Guinea and Australia
- **f** = feminine, **m** = masculine
- **sg** = singular, **pl** = plural

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Complex Coordination**: "X and Y" patterns with special verb agreement
2. **Gender System**: Masculine/feminine on nouns and verbs
3. **Number Agreement**: Singular vs. plural vs. dual
4. **Comitative Construction**: "with" constructions vs. coordination

## Key Patterns

### Coordination:
- Special markers for "X and Y" subjects
- Verb agrees with coordinated noun phrase
- Different patterns for dual vs. plural

### Gender Agreement:
- Verbs mark gender of subject/object
- Masculine vs. feminine distinctions
- Complex portmanteau morphemes

## Difficulty Analysis

**Level**: Intermediate-Advanced

**Reasoning**:
- Complex coordination patterns unusual cross-linguistically
- Gender and number interact in verb morphology
- Need to distinguish coordination from comitative
- Multiple interacting grammatical systems
