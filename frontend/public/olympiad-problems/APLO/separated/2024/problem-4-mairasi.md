## Problem Description

Here are some sentences in Mairasi and their English translations:

1. **omo obae avu nayomnai** - *I chased my grandfather.*
2. **eeme domba teosnyai** - *We sacrificed the sheep (sg).*
3. **sika nasai uratu imbiemnyai** - *The black cat dropped the fish (sg).*
4. **neme uratu neii navairjonnev** - *You (sg) caught the small fish (pl).*
5. **tat naii nabae nani navaseen bumbuemnyai** - *The small child kept hitting his tired father.*
6. **eme tat javiit mesionyev** - *You (pl) hid the children using the basket.*
7. **aasi nevwer domba nayemnyev** - *The big dogs chased the sheep (pl).*
8. **eme onggwasa teoiei** - *You (pl) cut (past) my hair.*
9. **eme nebae fuati avwer uvuoiei** - *You (pl) dipped their big net in the water.*
10. **nebae avu javii asai maimaitavemai** - *Your (sg) grandfather kept carrying the black basket.*
11. **ebae bembe newas namor buenggei** - *Your (pl) angry pigs hit (past) him in the face [literally: hit his face].*
12. **obae nani jerung narovot navairjemai** - *My father drew the bow [literally: pulled back the bow] using his hand (in preparation to shoot).*

**Note**: Sentences 9 and 10 can be translated into English in two different ways, but only one translation for each is provided here.

### Task (a)
Translate into English:

- 13. **omo domba nesai maitavomnev**
- 14. **neme eebae jerung imbionai**
- 15. **eeme eenggwasa eerovot uvuosei**
- 16. **omo fuati aii teonggai**

### Task (b)
The following sentence is grammatically incorrect:

- 17. **tat neii javii avwer imbiemai**

Explain why.

### Task (c)
Translate into Mairasi:

- 18. We sacrificed the big fish (pl).
- 19. I made your (sg) black dog swim.
- 20. You (pl) hid his grandfather using the net.
- 21. You (sg) kept hitting their small basket using your (sg) hand.

## Notes

- **ng** = ng in *king*
- **y** = y in *yet*
- **sg** = singular; **pl** = plural; **past** = past tense

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Complex Verb Agreement**: Verbs agree with both subject person/number and object number
2. **Aspect Marking**: Different verb forms for habitual/iterative vs. simple aspect
3. **Number Marking**: Singular vs. plural marking on nouns and in verb morphology
4. **Possessive Constructions**: Understanding possessive marking patterns
5. **Instrumental Marking**: How instruments are marked in the sentence structure

## Key Patterns to Discover

### Pronoun System:
- **1sg**: omo
- **2sg**: neme
- **3sg**: sika (independent), -(various forms) (possessive)
- **1pl**: eeme
- **2pl**: eme
- **3pl**: aasi (independent)

### Possessive Markers:
- **1sg possessive**: obae, aku
- **2sg possessive**: nebae, amiu (based on possessed noun class)
- **3sg possessive**: nabae, nona, bulana, nani
- **2pl possessive**: ebae

### Number Marking:
- **Singular objects**: Often indicated by specific verb forms
- **Plural objects**: Different verb morphology (e.g., -ev suffix patterns)
- **Adjective placement**: Adjectives like *neii* (small), *nevwer/avwer* (big), *nasai/asai* (black), *naii/aii* (small variant), *newas* (angry), *navaseen* (tired)

### Verb Morphology:
- **Aspect markers**:
  - Simple/perfective: -nai, -yai, -ei, -gai
  - Habitual/iterative (kept -ing): -emai, -nyai (different from simple)

- **Subject-Object agreement**: Complex portmanteau morphemes
  - Different verb forms depending on subject person/number AND object number

- **Causative/applicative**: Forms like "made X swim" (uvuosei type constructions)

### Verb Patterns:
- **Chase**: yom-, yem-
- **Sacrifice**: teos-, teong-
- **Drop**: imbiem-
- **Catch**: vairjon-
- **Hit**: buem-, bueng-
- **Hide**: mesion-
- **Cut**: teo-
- **Dip/make swim**: uvuo-
- **Carry**: maitav-, maitavom-

### Grammatical Constraints:
- Number agreement must be consistent between noun and verb
- Aspect marking affects verb stem choice
- Instrumental phrases ("using X") have specific syntax
