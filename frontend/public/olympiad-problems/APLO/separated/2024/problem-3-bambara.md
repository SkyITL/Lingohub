## Notes

- The words are given in phonetic transcription.
- **ʃ**, **ʒ**, **ʀ**, and **w** are consonants.
- **ɑ**, **ɔ**, **ɛ**, **ə**, **œ**, and **y** are vowels.
- The mark **~** indicates that the vowel is nasalised.

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Loanword Phonology**: Understanding how foreign sounds are adapted to native phonological constraints
2. **Phonotactic Constraints**: Recognizing what sound sequences are permitted in Bambara
3. **Systematic Sound Correspondences**: Identifying regular patterns of sound substitution
4. **Epenthesis**: Understanding vowel insertion to break up consonant clusters

## Key Patterns to Discover

### Sound Correspondences:
- **French /ʀ/ → Bambara /r/** (with vowel epenthesis in clusters)
- **French /v/ → Bambara /w/** (labial approximant)
- **French /ʃ/ → Bambara /s/** (fricative)
- **French /ʒ/ → Bambara /z/** (voiced fricative)
- **French /œ/ → Bambara /ɛ/** (vowel lowering)
- **French /y/ → Bambara /i/** (vowel unrounding)

### Phonotactic Adaptations:
- **Consonant cluster resolution**: French consonant clusters (especially with /ʀ/) are broken up with epenthetic vowels
  - /tʀ/ → /tVr/ (e.g., tʀɛ̃ → tɛrɛ̃, tʀaktœʀ → tarakitɛri)
  - /spɔʀ/ → /sipɔri/ (initial cluster also broken)
  - /pʀ/ → /pVr/ (e.g., pʀezidɑ̃ → perezidã)

- **Syllable structure preference**: Bambara prefers CV (consonant-vowel) syllables
- **Word-final vowels**: Bambara tends to add final vowels to French words ending in consonants
  - /ʀ/ → /ri/ or /rɛ/
  - /t/ → /ti/ or /tɛ/
  - /d/ → /di/

### Epenthetic Vowel Quality:
- The inserted vowel often copies or harmonizes with nearby vowels
- Common epenthetic vowels: /i/, /ɛ/, /a/, /o/, /u/

## Difficulty Analysis

- Some complexity in predicting epenthetic vowel quality
- Straightforward once patterns are identified, but requires careful analysis
