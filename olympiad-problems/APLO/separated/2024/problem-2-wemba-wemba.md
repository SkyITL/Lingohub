## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Metaphorical Extension**: Understanding how body parts are used metaphorically for other concepts
2. **Semantic Composition**: Recognizing how compound words combine meanings
3. **Cultural Semantics**: Understanding the conceptual mappings (e.g., head → top/roof, eye → opening/door)
4. **Morphological Patterns**: Identifying systematic compounding patterns

## Key Patterns to Discover

- **Body part metaphors**: Body parts serve as conceptual sources for other domains
  - Head (purrp) → Roof (top of house, like head is top of body)
  - Eye (katjin) → Door (opening, like eyes are openings)
  - Nose (kurrki) → Brain (inside the head)

- **Compound structure**: Modifier + Head patterns
  - Size/quality + body part
  - Body part + location/material

- **Age/size modifiers**:
  - Different terms for young/small vs. old/large animals
