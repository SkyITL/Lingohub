## Problem Description

Old Turkic words written in Old Turkic script with Roman transcriptions and English translations. Students must learn the script's spelling conventions.

### Tasks:
- (a) Write 6 words in the Old Turkic script (given Roman transcription)
- (b) Provide all possible Roman transcriptions of one Old Turkic script word
- (c) Describe the spelling rules of the Old Turkic script

## Notes

- **ä** ≈ e in "pet"
- **ï** is pronounced like u in "put", but without lip rounding
- **ö** and **ü** like German vowels (or French eu and u) - ä/i with rounded lips
- **q** and **ɣ** are like k and g, but further back in the mouth
- **č**, **š** and **ŋ** are consonants
- A **khagan** is an imperial ruler of some Turkic and Mongolic tribes

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Script Learning**: Deciphering an unfamiliar writing system
2. **Vowel Harmony**: Turkic vowel harmony reflected (or not) in orthography
3. **Underspelling**: Script may not represent all phonological distinctions
4. **Orthographic Conventions**: Systematic spelling rules to discover
5. **Ambiguity in Writing**: Part (b) asks for multiple possible readings

## Key Patterns

### Old Turkic Script Features:
- Ancient runic-like script used for Göktürk and Uyghur inscriptions
- Written right to left or left to right in different periods
- Distinctive angular characters

### Vowel Harmony in Turkic:
- **Front vowels**: ä, e, i, ö, ü
- **Back vowels**: a, ï, o, u
- Vowel harmony: vowels within word must harmonize
- Script may underspecify some vowel distinctions

### Spelling Rules (to discover):
Task (c) requires describing systematic orthographic patterns:
- How vowels are represented (or underrepresented)
- Consonant representation rules
- Word-final vs. word-medial patterns
- Possible underspelling of certain vowel contrasts
- Relationship between phonology and orthography

### Expected Discoveries:
- Script may not distinguish all vowel qualities
- Certain vowels may be omitted or merged in writing
- Consonant representation may be systematic but different from Roman
- Front/back harmony may affect spelling
