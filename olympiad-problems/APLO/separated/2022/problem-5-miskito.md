## Problem Description

In 1–5, all possible English translations for each Miskito sentence are given:

1. **ai kaiki banghwras kaisma** → *You (PL) won't see me.* / *You (PL) won't see us (1+3).*
2. **yawan man nani playkam kaikisa** → *We (1+2) see your (PL) post.*
3. **pulisna** → *I play.* / *We (1+3) play.*
4. **yawan ini banghwaisa** → *We (1+2) will cry.*
5. **araskam playa prukisa** → *Your (SG) horse hits the post.* / *Your (PL) horse hits the post.*

In 6–8, some or all possible English translations for each Miskito sentence are given:

6. **batana piakras** → *I don't cook the grease.* / *You (PL) won't cook the grease.*
7. **yang araski wan prukisa** → *My horse hits us (1+2).*
8. **aibipka pliki banghwaisna** → *We (1+3) will look for his cow.* / *We (1+3) will look for their cow.*

In 9–11, some or all possible Miskito translations for each English sentence are given:

9. *You (PL) cook his snake.* → **man nani aipyutka piaki banghwisma** / **aipyutka piaki banghwisma** / **witin aipyutka piakisma**
10. *They don't cry.* → **witin nani ini banghwras** / **witin nani ini banghwras sa** / **inras sa**
11. *You (SG) will look for our (1+2) post.* → **man wanplayka plikaisma**

In 12–13, all possible Miskito translations for each English sentence are given:

12. *He detests you (PL).* → **mai kulkisa** / **witin mai kulkisa**
13. *We (1+3) will hit him.* → **prukaisna** / **yang nani prukaisna** / **pruki banghwaisna** / **yang nani pruki banghwaisna**

### Task (a)
Translate into English in all possible ways:

14. **man wanaraska piakras sma**
15. **witin aiaraska kaikaisa**
16. **pyuta kulki banghwras kaisa**

### Task (b)
For each of the sentences below write out all Miskito translations that contain the word given in brackets:

17. *I don't look for you (SG).* **(yang)**
18. *We (1+3) will not see them.* **(nani)**
19. *We (1+2) will detest your (PL) snake.* **(man)**
20. *You (PL) hit [present tense] our (1+2) grease.* **(yawan)**

## Notes

- **We (1+2)** = you (PL) and I (inclusive)
- **We (1+3)** = they and I (exclusive)
- **SG** = singular; **PL** = plural

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Clusivity**: Understanding inclusive (1+2) vs. exclusive (1+3) first person plural
2. **Optional Marking**: Some grammatical markers can be omitted, creating ambiguity
3. **Complex Agreement**: Subject and object person/number marking on verbs
4. **Pronoun System**: Full vs. reduced pronominal forms

## Key Patterns to Discover

### Pronouns:
- **1sg**: ai (object), yang (possessive/subject)
- **2sg**: man (possibly)
- **3sg**: witin
- **1pl (incl, 1+2)**: yawan
- **1pl (excl, 1+3)**: yang nani (optionally)
- **2pl**: man nani / mai
- **3pl**: witin nani

### Nouns:
- playkam / playa / playka = post
- araski / araska = horse
- batana = grease
- aipyutka = snake
- aibipka = cow
- pyuta = (unknown animal)

### Verbs:
- kaiki = see
- kaikisa = see (with different subject/object)
- puli- = play
- ini = cry
- pruki- = hit
- piaki- = cook
- pliki- = look for
- kulki- = detest

### Tense/Aspect/Negation:
- **-sna**: 1pl.excl (1+3) subject marker OR future
- **-sa**: future
- **-isa**: present/general
- **bangh-**: future marker
- **-ras**: negative
- **-kra**: negative (variant)

### Person/Number Marking:

**Subject marking** (on verbs):
- **1sg**: -na (sometimes), -Ø
- **2sg**: -sma (sometimes)
- **3sg**: -Ø, witin (optional pronoun)
- **1pl.incl (1+2)**: yawan (pronoun) + verb
- **1pl.excl (1+3)**: -sna (suffix) OR yang nani (pronoun)
- **2pl**: -sma OR man nani / mai
- **3pl**: witin nani

**Object marking**:
- **1sg**: ai (pronoun)
- **2sg**: Ø
- **1pl.incl**: wan
- **1pl.excl**: Ø (or yang nani)
- **2pl**: Ø OR man nani

### Optional Marking System:

The key difficulty in this problem is that many markers are **optional**:

1. **Subject pronouns** can be omitted if the verb has subject agreement
2. **Possessive pronouns** can be omitted in some contexts
3. **Number markers** (nani) can sometimes be omitted
4. This creates **ambiguity** - one form can have multiple interpretations

Examples of ambiguity:
- **pulisna** = "I play" OR "We (1+3) play" (both use -sna)
- **araskam playa prukisa** = "Your (SG) horse..." OR "Your (PL) horse..." (number not marked on possessive)
- **ai kaiki banghwras kaisma** = "You (PL) won't see me" OR "...us (1+3)" (object number ambiguous)

### Verb Conjugation Patterns:

**Full paradigm** (example: puli- "play"):
- 1sg: pulisna (ambiguous with 1pl.excl)
- 2sg: puli + ?
- 3sg: puli + ?
- 1pl.incl (1+2): yawan puli + ?
- 1pl.excl (1+3): pulisna OR yang nani puli + ?
- 2pl: man nani puli + ? OR mai puli + ?
- 3pl: witin nani puli + ?

**Tense marking**:
- Present: -isa
- Future: -sa, bangh- ... -sa
- Negative: -ras, banghwras

### Key Observations:

1. **Inclusive vs. Exclusive**:
   - **yawan** = we (1+2) = you and I (inclusive)
   - **yang nani** = we (1+3) = they and I (exclusive)
   - **-sna** can mark either 1sg OR 1pl.excl!

2. **Optional "nani"** (plural marker):
   - Can be added to pronouns to mark plurality
   - **man** = you (SG or PL ambiguously)
   - **man nani** = you (PL) explicitly
   - **witin** = he/she
   - **witin nani** = they

3. **Object incorporation**:
   - Objects are often incorporated into the verb
   - **wan** = us (1+2) (inclusive)
   - **mai** = you (PL)

4. **Alternative forms**:
   - Multiple ways to express same meaning
   - Can use full pronoun + verb, or just verb with agreement
   - **witin mai kulkisa** = **mai kulkisa** = "He detests you (PL)"

## Difficulty Analysis

**Level**: Advanced

**Reasoning**:
- Clusivity distinction is uncommon and conceptually challenging
- Extensive optional marking creates significant ambiguity
- Need to identify when forms are truly ambiguous vs. when context disambiguates
- Complex interaction between pronouns, agreement, and word order
- Multiple alternative ways to express the same meaning
- Requires building complete paradigm across many dimensions
- One of the most complex morphosyntactic systems in APLO history
