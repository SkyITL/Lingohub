## Problem Description

Words with stress marks showing primary (́) and secondary (̀) stress. Task: Place 24 missing stress marks on unstressed words.

## Notes

- **č**, **ŋ**, **š**, **z̨**, **ž̨** and **ʔ** are consonants
- **ɨ** is a vowel
- **ː** denotes a long vowel
- A **willow blind** is equipment for bird-hunting

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Weight-Sensitive Stress**: Stress depends on syllable weight
2. **Primary vs. Secondary Stress**: Two levels of stress prominence
3. **Syllable Structure**: Understanding heavy vs. light syllables
4. **Predictable Stress**: Stress assignment rules based on phonological structure

## Key Patterns

### Syllable Weight:
- **Heavy syllables**: CVː (long vowel), CVC (closed syllable)
- **Light syllables**: CV (open with short vowel)

### Stress Assignment Rules:
- Primary stress on specific heavy syllables
- Secondary stress on alternating syllables
- Distance from word edge matters
- Predictable patterns based on syllable weight

### Expected Pattern:
- Weight-sensitive system where heavy syllables attract stress
- Possible iambic or trochaic rhythm
- Primary stress on particular position
- Secondary stress fills in rhythmically
