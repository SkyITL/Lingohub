## Problem Description

Given below are verb forms of the Georgian language (in Roman transcription) and their English translations in arbitrary order:

### Georgian Forms
vtkvi, kenit, inadiret, itavmGdomareve, vsadilobt, tkvi, vigoreb, vkeni, nadirob, visadileb, vinadire, ambob, vitavmGdomareve, izamt, vivlit

### English Translations
you say, we dine, you hunt, I said, you (pl.) did, I did, you said, I hunted, we will walk, I presided, I will roll, I will dine, you (pl.) hunted, you presided, you (pl.) will do

### Task

**§§.** Determine the correct correspondences. If you find more than one solution possible, indicate which one you think is more plausible and explain why.

## Notes

- G is a consonant pronounced as the first or the last sound in *judge*.

## Linguistic Insights

### Key Patterns

1. **Person Marking**:
   - First person: v- prefix
   - Second person: different patterns for singular/plural
   - Third person: unmarked or different patterns

2. **Number Marking**:
   - Singular vs. plural distinctions
   - Plural may be marked in multiple ways

3. **Tense/Aspect**:
   - Past, present, future distinctions
   - Different stem forms for different tenses

4. **Verb Stems**:
   - Multiple roots represent different verbs
   - Stems may change based on tense/aspect

### Difficulty Analysis

**Difficulty Level**: 3/5 (Moderate-Challenging)

**Challenge Areas**:
- Segmenting verbs into morphemes
- Distinguishing person vs. number markers
- Identifying tense markers
- Handling potential homophony or syncretism

**Solution Approach**:
1. Group forms by apparent similarity
2. Identify consistent patterns for person/number
3. Look for tense/aspect markers
4. Match patterns to translations
5. Verify consistency and handle ambiguities
