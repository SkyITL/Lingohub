## Problem Description

Here are several Khmer words written in a special variety of the traditional Khmer alphabet (the so-called 'Round Script'), their English translations, as well as Roman transcriptions of these words in arbitrary order.

### Given Data:
13 Khmer words in Round Script with English translations, and their romanizations in scrambled order:
**poьm, a:c, noьm, ŋoьm, cam, peьŋ, aŋ, caŋ, am, piъm, ŋiъk, ka:m, miъn**

### Tasks:
- **Assignment 1**: Determine the correct correspondences.
- **Assignment 2**: Write 6 new words in the Roman script.

## Notes

- a, eь, oь are short vowels
- a:, iъ are long vowels
- ŋ, c are consonants
- Cham is an ethnic group in Southeast Asia

## Linguistic Insights

This problem tests:
1. **Script Analysis**: Understanding how Khmer Round Script represents sounds
2. **Phonological Patterns**: Recognizing systematic sound-symbol correspondences
3. **Vowel Length**: Distinguishing short vs. long vowels
4. **Syllable Structure**: Understanding CV, CVC patterns

## Key Patterns

### Khmer Round Script Features:
- Abugida writing system (consonant-vowel combinations)
- Vowel diacritics modify consonant symbols
- Systematic representation of vowel length
- Consistent consonant symbols

### Phonological Structure:
- Syllable structure: (C)V(C)
- Vowel length distinction (short: eь, oь vs. long: a:, iъ)
- Special consonants: ŋ (velar nasal), c (voiceless palatal)
